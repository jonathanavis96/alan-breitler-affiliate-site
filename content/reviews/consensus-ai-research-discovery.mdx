## Overview

Consensus is an AI-powered academic search engine designed to help researchers, students, and academics quickly discover and synthesize findings from peer-reviewed literature. Unlike traditional databases like PubMed or Google Scholar, Consensus uses large language models to extract key results from papers and present them as digestible, evidence-based answers.

We evaluated Consensus across our five scoring dimensions to help you decide whether it fits your research workflow.

## Key Features

- **Consensus Meter** — Aggregates findings across multiple studies to show the overall scientific consensus on a given query
- **AI Summaries** — Generates concise, citation-backed summaries from relevant papers
- **Citation Export** — One-click export to BibTeX, RIS, and popular reference managers
- **Filters** — Narrow results by study design, journal, sample size, and publication date

## Accuracy (8.5/10)

Consensus generally surfaces relevant, high-quality papers. The AI-generated summaries are well-grounded in the source material, though we occasionally noticed minor mischaracterisations of nuanced findings. For established topics with large bodies of literature, accuracy is excellent.

## Ease of Use (9.0/10)

The interface is remarkably clean. Type a research question in natural language and receive structured results almost instantly. There is virtually no learning curve — a significant advantage over traditional systematic review workflows.

## Feature Depth (7.5/10)

While the core search-and-synthesis loop is polished, power users may find the filtering options limited compared to specialised databases. There is no API for programmatic access, and batch queries are not yet supported.

## Academic Relevance (9.5/10)

This is where Consensus truly shines. The tool was purpose-built for academic research, and it shows. The consensus meter and study-level metadata make it invaluable for literature reviews, grant writing, and evidence synthesis.

## Value for Money (8.0/10)

The free tier provides enough queries and features for exploratory research. The premium plan (from $9.99/mo with academic discount) unlocks full-text access, advanced filters, and higher query limits — a reasonable investment for active researchers.
